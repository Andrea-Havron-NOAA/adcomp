6 ## Notation
7 
8 We use the following notation
9 
10 | Notation | Explanation |
11 |---------------------|------------------------------------------------------|
12 | \f$u\f$ | The random effects vector |
13 | \f$\theta\f$ | Parameter vector (first part) |
14 | \f$\beta\f$ | Parameter vector (second part) |
15 | \f$f(u,\beta,\theta)\f$ | Joint negative log likelihood |
16 | \f$x\f$ | Data |
17 | \f$E(u|x)\f$ | Conditional expectation of random effect given data |
18 | \f$\hat u\f$ | The posterior mode \f$\arg \min_{u} f(u,\beta,\theta)\f$ |
19 
20 ## Profiling the inner problem
21 
22 This section describes the underlying theory of the argument `profile`
23 to `MakeADFun` intended to speedup and robustify linear mixed effect
24 models with a large number of fixed effects. With a few common model
25 properties (Assumption 1 and 2 below), which must be checked by the
26 user, one can apply the `profile` argument to move outer parameters to
27 the inner problem without affecting the model result.
28 
29 **Theorem 1 (Profiling inner problem)**
30 Assume that for any \f$\beta\f$ and \f$\theta\f$
31 
32 * **Assumption 1** The partial derivative \f$\partial_{\beta} f(u,\beta,\theta)\f$ is a linear function of u.
33 * **Assumption 2** The posterior mean is equal to the posterior mode: \f$E(u|x)=\hat u\f$
34 
35 Then the MLE
36 
37 \f[\hat \beta := \arg \max_{\beta} \left( \int \exp(-f(u,\beta,\theta)) \: du \right) \f]
38 
39 is a solution to the augmented system
40 
41 \f[
42 \begin{split}
43 \partial_{u} f(u,\beta,\theta) &= 0 \\
44 \partial_{\beta} f(u,\beta,\theta) &= 0
45 \end{split}
46 \f]
47 
48 The augmented system defines \f$\hat \beta\f$ implicitly as function of the posterior mode \f$\hat u\f$.
49 
50 *Proof*
51 
52 Differentiation of the negative log marginal likelihood gives
53 
54 \f[
55 \begin{split}
56 \partial_{\beta} \left( -\log \int \exp(-f(u,\beta,\theta)) \: du \right) &= E(\partial_{\beta}f(u,\beta,\theta) |x) \\
57 &= \partial_{\beta} f(u,\beta,\theta)_{|u=\hat u(\beta,\theta)}
58 \end{split}
59 \f]
60 
61 where the first equality holds in general and the second equality follows from assumptions (1) and (2).
62 
63 \f$\square\f$
64 
65 ## Example
66 
67 The standard situation for which **assumption 1** holds is when the
68 \f$\beta\f$s are the linear fixed effects of a mixed model. In this case
69 the joint negative log density takes the form
70 \f[ f(u,\beta,\theta) = \frac{1}{2}(u-A\beta)'\Sigma_{\theta}^{-1}(u-A\beta) + ... \f]
71 for some design matrix \f$A\f$ where ' \f$...\f$ ' does not depend on
72 \f$\beta\f$. The derivative
73 \f[ \partial_{\beta} f(u,\beta,\theta) = A'\Sigma_{\theta}^{-1}(u-A\beta) \f]
74 is thus a linear function of the random effect \f$u\f$.
75 
76 In general **assumption 2** holds exact for models with a symmetric
77 (e.g. Gaussian) posterior distribution.
